Titolo & Process parameters tuning and online re-slicing for robotized Additive Manufacturing of big plastic objects
Autori & Rebaioli, L.; Magnoni, P.; Fassi, I.; Pedrocchi, N.; Molinari Tosatti, L.;
Ruolo & Autore alla pari
Rivista & Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing (JCR, Scopus, Scimago)
Anno & 2019
Pagine & 55-64, 55(A)
ISSN & 0736-5845
doi & 10.1016/j.rcim.2018.07.012
Abstract & Complex parts can be successfully manufactured using Additive Manufacturing (AM) techniques based on thermoplastic polymer extrusion, whose use for mass production is restricted by their slow printing speed. A flexible AM platform for big plastic objects has been realized to address this limitation by mounting an industrial screw-based extruder on an anthropomorphic robot. An experimental campaign has been performed to set a suitable range of relevant process parameters to ensure a regular deposited layer geometry. Moreover, a closed-loop control strategy has been developed to correct the robot height based on data measured during the material deposition, thus further improving the process parameter setting and compensating the material shrinkage or other unexpected defects. Eventually, an online re-slicing algorithm was implemented to preserve the desired height of the manufactured object, despite the layer height changes. ...
