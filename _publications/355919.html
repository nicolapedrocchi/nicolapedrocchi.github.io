Titolo & Optimal impedance force-tracking control design with impact formulation for interaction tasks

Abstract & The letter presents a force-tracking impedance controller granting a free-overshoots contact force (mandatory performance for many critical interaction tasks such as polishing) for partially unknown interacting environments (such as leather or hard-fragile materials). As in many applications, the robot has to gently approach the target environment (whose position is usually not well-known), then execute the interaction task. Therefore, the algorithm has been designed to handle both the free space approaching motion and the succeeding contact task without switching from different control logics. Control gains must be calculated appropriately for each phase to achieve the target force-tracking performance. In detail, phase a. control gains are optimized based on the impact collision model to minimize the force error during the following contact task ....
