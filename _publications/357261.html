Titolo & Exploiting impedance shaping approaches to overcome force overshoots in delicate interaction tasks

Abstract & The presented article aims to overcome the force overshoot issue in impedance-based force tracking applications. Nowadays, lightweight manipulators are involved in high-accurate force control applications (such as polishing tasks), where the force overshoot issue is critical (i.e., damaging the component and causing a production waste), exploiting the impedance control. The literature describes two main force-tracking impedance control approaches: (a) set-point deformation and (b) variable stiffness approaches. However, no contributions are directly related to the force overshoot issue. The presented article extends both such methodologies to analytically achieve force overshoot avoidance in interaction tasks based on the online estimation of the interacting environment stiffness (available through an EKF).
